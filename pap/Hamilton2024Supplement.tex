\section{Ocean2k Cooling Rate Estimation \label{sec:oc2k}}
We computed the Ocean2k anomaly trend from 800-1800 CE by binning the records into 200 year bins, centered on the years 800-1800 CE. Uncertainty was quantified by the standard deviation of all values for a particular record that fell into a single bin. If there was only one data point within a bin, an uncertainty of 0 was assigned. A mean global time series was calculated by computing the average of all binned records that have data for each bin. Then, the linear least squares slope was computed. For all 57 records, this was computed to be -0.47 $\pm$ 0.1 $^{\circ}$C/kyr. \cite{ocean2k} reports this value as -0.42 $^{\circ}$C/kyr. For the subset of 28 North Atlantic records, this was computed to be -0.36 $\pm$ 0.12 $^{\circ}$C/kyr.

\subsection{Solution Comparison to Instrumental Record}
We can compute time-varying surface maps of temperature and $\delta^{18}\mathrm{O}_{\mathrm{seawater}}$ via Equation (\ref{main-eq:surfrecon}), which is shown in Figure \ref{main-fig:sst}. Although observational time series on relevant timescales are rare, we average the SST map in a Labrador Sea box, and compare it to a comparable region in the Hadley Centre Sea Ice and Sea Surface Temperature dataset (HadISST) \cite[]{Rayner-Parker-2003:Global}, as well as a digitized long-term Labrador Sea Water observational dataset \cite[]{LSG--1998:Labrador} in Figure \ref{fig:labcomp}. An approximation of salinity can be arrived at from our $\delta^{18}\mathrm{O}_{\mathrm{seawater}}$ reconstruction by using the ratio between salinity and $\delta^{18}\mathrm{O}_{\mathrm{seawater}}$ changes of 0.5255 \cite{marchitto}, which can be compared to the long-term salinity record from \citeA{LSG--1998:Labrador}.

\begin{figure}[htbp]
\centerline{\includegraphics[width = \textwidth]{/home/brynn/Code/OPTinv.jl/plots/labcompallvold.png}}
\caption[]{\label{fig:labcomp} \textbf{(A)} Reconstructed Labrador Sea (60$^{\circ}$W-30$^{\circ}$E, 55$^{\circ}$N-64$^{\circ}$N) temperature and \textbf{(B)} salinity (computed as $0.5255 \times \delta^{18}\mathrm{O}_{\mathrm{seawater}}$) over the instrumental period (1850-1970) is shown for \texttt{old} (blue) and \texttt{all} (red) solutions. Solid line shows digitized Labrador Sea record \cite{LSG--1998:Labrador}. Dashed line shows winter HadISST \cite{Rayner-Parker-2003:Global} Labrador Sea spatial mean (60$^{\circ}$W-30$^{\circ}$E, 55$^{\circ}$N-64$^{\circ}$N). All records are shown as an anomaly from 1970.}
\end{figure}

We expect some smoothing in our solution reconstruction, as a result of using benthic records which do not experience near-surface variability nor seasonal variations. This is evident in both the temperature and salinity panels in Figure \ref{fig:labcomp}. We note that the magnitude of changes in reconstructed temperature and salinity are within the range of decadal-scale variability for the sparse instrumental records in our region of interest.
